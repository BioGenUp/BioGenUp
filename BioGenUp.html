About the Program

Our Biotech Internship is an intensive short‑term training pathway designed for students and early‑career learners who want practical exposure to modern laboratory methods, data analysis, and biotechnology research culture. Over 8 weeks, interns rotate through skill modules, complete a guided mini‑project, and present their findings.

  • Wet lab basics: pipetting, aseptic technique, culture handling.
  • Molecular biology workflows: DNA isolation, PCR, gel analysis.
  • Data literacy: basic bioinformatics & visualization.
  • Career mentoring & graduate study guidance.

Eligibility

We welcome motivated learners from life‑science backgrounds. You do not need prior lab experience—just curiosity and commitment.

  • Current student or graduate in biotech, biology, biochem, pharmacy, or related fields.
  • Basic understanding of cell & molecular biology concepts.
  • Comfort with following lab safety instructions.
  • Ability to commit to the full internship duration.

Sample Mini‑Projects

Interns work in small teams with mentor guidance. Projects change each cohort—here are examples.

CRISPR Target Analysis (in‑silico)

Use genome databases to identify candidate CRISPR guide sites and evaluate off‑target risk.

PCR Detection of GMO Markers

Design primers, amplify target sequences, and visualize results on agarose gel.

Microbial Growth Curves

Measure OD600 over time to model growth kinetics under different nutrient conditions.

Protein Expression & SDS‑PAGE

Induce recombinant protein expression, purify, and analyze band pattern.

Basic Sequence Alignment Workshop

Compare gene sequences and interpret alignment scores with BLAST.

Intro to Bioinformatics Pipelines

Get a taste of NGS data QC and simple variant calling workflow (demo dataset).
